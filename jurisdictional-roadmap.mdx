## Backlog Loan Demand

SALT captures demand signals from users in jurisdictions where lending is not yet available. When a user in an unsupported jurisdiction submits a loan request, they enter the **loan demand queue** — a backlog that informs licensing prioritization.

### How Demand Signals Work

1. User signs up for a SALT account (no KYC required at signup)
2. Selects Personal or Business loan type on the Marketplace
3. Completes loan calculator and submits
4. SALT records the jurisdiction and loan parameters
5. Demand data feeds into licensing expansion priorities

<Warning>
Jurisdictions on the **ineligible list** (OFAC-sanctioned countries, high-risk regions) are permanently excluded regardless of demand. See the [full ineligible list](https://saltlending.com/ineligible-jurisdictions/).
</Warning>

## US Regulatory Landscape

Crypto-backed lending in the US requires navigating a patchwork of state licensing regimes:

- **Money Transmitter Licenses (MTLs)** are required in most states for crypto activities, with costs ranging from $375 (Florida) to $15,000+ (New York)
- **Surety bonds** range from $10,000 to $7M depending on state and transaction volume
- **New York BitLicense** remains the most rigorous regime with 12-24 month processing times
- **California DFAL** (Digital Financial Assets Law) requires licensing by July 2026 for digital asset activities
- **Montana** is the only state that does not require a money transmitter license

## Expansion Priorities

Licensing expansion is prioritized by:

1. **Backlog demand volume** — jurisdictions with highest queued loan requests
2. **Regulatory clarity** — markets with established crypto-lending frameworks
3. **Strategic fit** — alignment with segment growth (e.g., enterprise-heavy markets for Phase 2-3)
4. **Cost-to-license** — bond requirements, application fees, and processing timelines
5. **Partner distribution** — markets where API/white-label partners operate
